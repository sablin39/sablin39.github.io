A B O U T T H E A U T H O R S                                             


Ludwig Chincarini is a Professor of Finance in the School of Management at the University of San Francisco. He is also a financial consultant and is Director of Quantitative Strategies at United States Commodity Funds. He has taught derivatives, investments, quanti- tative portfolio management, hedge funds, and private equity to both undergraduates and MBA students. His previous academic position was adjunct professor of finance at Georgetown University. Prior to this, he was an academic advisor to IndexIQ helping to design indices and ETFs and the founding member of the academic counsel of Futureadvisor. He was also director of research at the index mutual fund company Rydex Global Advisors (now Guggenheim investments), where he codeveloped the new S&P 500 Equal-Weight Index in collaboration with Standard & Poor’s. He also served a key role in launching the first equal-weight S&P 500 ETF. Before that he served as director of research for FOLIOfn. FOLIOfn was a revo- lutionary new brokerage firm that was one of the first brokerages to offer basket trading. Dr. Chincarini helped to build the order- processing system, he aided programmers with all the financial cal- culations on the Web site, he helped design and develop the portfolio management system to manage over 100 portfolios for customers, he led the effort on new ideas and new products, and he and his research group developed all the financial content on the Web site. He served a key role in developing many of the firm patents that were filed with the U.S. Patent Office and have since been approved. The company won numerous awards, including the 2001 CIO Web Business 50, the 2001 Forbes Favorite, and the 2001 Red Herring Top 100 awards.

658 About the Authors


Prior to building FOLIOfn, Dr. Chincarini worked in Switzerland, where he served in the portfolio management group of the banking department of the Bank for International Settlements (BIS). This is the so-called central bank of central banks. The research developed by his group was used by central banks, as well as the BIS, to make strategic investment decisions. He also managed a fixed-income portfolio on the bank’s behalf. Dr. Chincarini has written papers and given seminars on a wide range of issues, including asset allocation, interest-rate forecasting, global fixed-income management, and efficient markets. An invest- ment newsletter that he developed at the BIS featured guest essays from the leading academics in finance on a variety of topics, including market bubbles, the future of gold prices, and risk man- agement. Prior to the BIS, Dr. Chincarini worked in the investment research department of Schroders, a highly prestigious equity firm. He graduated from the University of California at Berkeley summa cum laude, earning a B.A. in economics. He also holds a Ph.D. in economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.


Daehwan Kim is a professor at Konkuk University in South Korea. Prior to that he was a portfolio manager at First Private Investment Management. He has taught econometrics, statistics, financial economics, and public finance to undergraduate and graduate students at Ewha Women’s University, Korea University, Nizhny Novgorod University, Konkuk University, and the American University in Bulgaria. Before becoming an academic, he worked for FOLIOfn as a financial economist, helping to create innovative financial products. He also worked for a business newspaper covering financial and other news and had regular columns on financial economics in a business weekly. He has coauthored two books on public finance. Dr. Kim earned a B.A. in economics from Seoul National University and a Ph.D. in economics from Harvard University. He is a recipient of the Yeonam Foundation Fellowship and of the Korea Foundation for Advanced Studies Fellowship, sponsored by LG Corp. and SK Corp.
