11.1 INTRODUCTION

A study by James Garland on taxable investing concluded that, “Those who stand to lose the least—to taxes and fees—stand to win the most when the game’s all over.”1 In the game of investing, the winners are the ones who know not only how to earn a lot but also how to hold on to what they earn. Many investment managers have forgotten to apply this seemingly obvious lesson. Garland’s study showed that from 1971 to 1995, the typical mutual fund lost approximately 44% of its gross return to the government in taxes. The typical fund manager undoubtedly put plenty of effort into managing his or her fund, but he or she got to keep only a little over half of what he or she earned for his or her efforts.

By devising a solid tax management strategy, the typical man- ager could have kept more earnings in the portfolio and out of Uncle Sam’s reach. Taxes are by no means a constant in the investment world.
