9A.3 QUADRATIC PROGRAMMING WITH INEQUALITY CONSTRAINTS

The quadratic optimization problem with inequality constraints cannot be solved with a closed-form solution. The solution is obtained through numerical solution methods. Numerical solu- tions have become much more reliable and feasible over the past 20 years owing to the increased power of computers and some advances in linear and quadratic programming. The most typical method that is used is the active-set method or projection method. Other methods are used as well, including the interior-point method.10 In this section we will describe the active-set method and briefly describe the interior-point method.11
